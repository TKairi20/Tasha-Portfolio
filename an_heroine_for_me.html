An Heroïne For Me
Artist Statement

This is a typography collage of Wonder Woman that I made using Adobe Photoshop. I wanted to make a collage based on the main theme of "Feminist Film Theory" and the reason I chose Wonder Woman is because to me, it’s my first ever strong female character I have seen in movies. She was also my favorite hero at a young age, which is why she became the first option when I was trying to choose what female character to me would represent this project.

It’s my first ever typography collage and I actually had a lot of fun doing this portrait. Not only is it one of my favorite projects I have done so far personally, but also it was a collaborative project. Collaborative in the way that every word I put in this collage were words that my friends expressed when I asked them “what feminism film theory represents to you”. Some expressed definitions while others expressed their opinions. With all those words combined, it gave a full art piece as a result.

In a way, I was inspired by my Interactive Art class, where instead of it just being an art piece, the viewer gets to interact with it. So this piece is a result of all the perspectives and different points of views that different people had on the subject.

I chose the title “An Heroïne For Me” because I felt like it fitted the theme. To me, there’s no better way to describe how a female character should be written than looking at every view of different types of people. And I think that’s also one of the messages of this movement, because in the past women didn’t have the chance to express themselves as much (from directors to acting). But now we get to see so many movies directed by women who are as exceptional as movies directed by men.

To me, Cinema is a sort of “virtual” reality where you get to become a part of that new reality. So of course I value the importance of representation, whether it’s gender or cultures. Which is what gave the result to my collage and is also the reason why this project is meaningful to me.
