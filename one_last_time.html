One Last Time

Artist Statement

This is my first ever animation project that I made in the class Animation Atelier in Fall 2024. I used the program Procreate and Adobe Premiere Pro to make it.

Although I am not a big fan of drawing, I had a lot of fun trying out the different textures offered by Procreate (especially the ones for the backgrounds). For this project, I had the idea of doing multiple layers in which I assembled them using Adobe Premiere Pro. What I did is I separated the characters (or moving objects and animals) in one layer and the background in a second layer. That way, I could animate the characters peacefully. It was the first time for me to use Procreate or to animate, so I used my skills in editing videos to help me with the making of this project.

The message I wanted to convey is to move on with no regret, in which the color red symbolizes ‘death’ in this context. I did not want to put a lot of colors because, to me, I wanted this short animation to have a grayish atmosphere since the main theme is “death”. I emphasized a lot with the umbrella being red so that the color could “pop off” and I felt like this object was the perfect symbolism I could put in there. Of course, what I would love to improve in this project is the way I conveyed the story. It may seem a bit confusing in certain points, and thus I want to make it even more direct.

The reason why I chose this message is because I wanted to express how important it is to be grateful of what we have and to make the most of it because we never know when we will all have to leave. Because the moment we pass away, we won’t have the chance to come back to do everything we wanted to do. I want to show that what you can do presently should not negatively impact your future. To not have any “regrets” when our time comes.
