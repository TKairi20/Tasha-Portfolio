Crying Woman

Artist Statement

This is a project I have made in my class Exploring Creativity.

At first, I wanted to make a collage of multiple magazines and make the face of a person with them, but then I wanted to explore more and since I had just learned the basics in Adobe Photoshop, I wanted to be creative and make my first digital collage instead. I already worked a few years back in high school in a program called Photopea (A similar program to Photoshop, but instead for free and online), so it wasn’t too hard for me to familiarize myself with the tools.

For the making of this project, I decided to download digital magazines and then combine them to make a human form. I also wanted to explore with the colors, so I separated sections of the portraits (hair, shirt, tears and face) and put a specific color in each section, making it a bit more alive in a way (as shown just below this paragraph).

I did not have a specific theme for this project when I was making it. Instead, I preferred letting the viewer decide what is the actual meaning of the art. I used multiple colors that are quite distinct from themselves because in my opinion, art can have different types of definition for each person. The green or red color could make the digital art seem hopeful whereas the blue could make it seem a bit sadder. All depends on the viewer’s perspective. There is also no specific name I have given to this art piece, so I decided to go with a basic one, which is “Crying Woman”.

This is a special project to me because it was my first official digital art piece that I have made by exploring a completely new medium. If I had to redo it again, I would love to put in even more colors and details to challenge myself even more.
