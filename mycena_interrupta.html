Mycena Interrupta

Blender Modelling

Artist Statement

I used Blender for this project, which was my first time doing 3D modelling. I learned about it years ago, but had a hard time understanding it. I wanted to challenge myself because one of my goals is to become a video game developer, and I also want to be able to create my own assets.

I based myself on the blue Mycena Interrupta (a type of mushroom) to create an environment that is “realistic” just as much as fantasy-like. I wanted to create an environment that felt ethereal using this mushroom. The reason why I chose this specific mushroom is because of a commemoration of a past project of mine, where I had to sculpt a mushroom. Since I was never given back the project, I decided to commemorate it and put it in 3D in a more artistic way.

I modelled the mesh using the Object-Edit Mode and used the Nodes to put textures on those objects, like the gradient texture on my mushrooms and the image textures for the walls (which I downloaded for free on ambientCG). I wanted to put it in a sort of “box”, since I didn’t want to make something too complex and wanted to put the mushroom in a sort of cave. In my opinion, what made the result better was the lightning. I wanted to go with a little bluish color to give a soothing feeling to the environment. I also am a big fan of fantasy, which is why I wanted to customize my mushroom a little bit more to make it look more surrealistic. I learned a lot about modelling, textures, lighting and modifiers during this project, how ambience is important to express a "feeling" with the environment.

In terms of improvement, I would say I could have improved the rendering of the textures. It is something I want to learn more about, since when I tried to render an animation, it was taking too much time for my own device. I want to learn how to optimize my project in a way that it will still look smooth and realistic. Especially if I want to create future assets for some video games.
